STUDENT SUCCESS BLUEPRINT



STEP 1: BLUEPRINT OUTPUT FRAMEWORK



OUTPUT 1: OVERALL STUDENT PROFILE

Purpose

Provides a high-level summary of the student's overall personality, learning preferences, strengths, interests, and potential.

This serves as the student's complete profile overview.



OUTPUT 2: TOP STRENGTHS

Purpose

Identifies the student's strongest natural abilities, traits, and advantages.

These are the areas where the student is most likely to perform naturally and confidently.



OUTPUT 3: HIDDEN STRENGTHS

Purpose

Identifies strengths that may not currently be fully developed but show strong future potential.

These strengths may emerge with experience, learning, and exposure.



OUTPUT 4: POTENTIAL BLIND SPOTS

Purpose

Identifies tendencies, behaviors, or weaknesses that may limit future growth if left unaddressed.

These are developmental areas rather than weaknesses.



OUTPUT 5: LEARNING ENVIRONMENT FIT

Purpose

Identifies the environments, teaching methods, and learning conditions in which the student is most likely to succeed.

This helps students, parents, and educators understand how the student learns best.



OUTPUT 6: MOTIVATION DRIVERS

Purpose

Identifies the factors that naturally increase the student's engagement, effort, persistence, and satisfaction.

Understanding motivation helps improve learning, performance, and goal achievement.



OUTPUT 7: FUTURE POTENTIAL THEMES

Purpose

Identifies the student's dominant future potential themes and natural pathways.

These themes represent how the student is likely to create value, contribute, solve problems, and succeed in the future.



OUTPUT 8: PERSONALIZED GROWTH ROADMAP

Purpose

Provides practical recommendations and development suggestions for personal growth, learning, skills, and future readiness.

This converts insights into action.



OUTPUT 9: FUTURE READINESS SCORE

Purpose

Provides an overall indicator of the student's readiness for future learning, adaptability, growth, and changing career demands.



OUTPUT 10: FUTURE SUCCESS INDEX

Purpose

Provides a summary indicator of the student's overall potential for growth, achievement, learning, and long-term success.

STEP 4B: THEME LOGIC

This step defines the rules that govern how the 22 dimension scores are combined into the 12 Career Theme Scores, the Future Readiness Score, and the Future Success Index in Step 4A and Step 3. These rules apply uniformly across every formula in this document.



1. Normalization Rule

Every dimension score must be converted to a 0–100 Normalized Score before it is used in any Theme Score, Career Fit Score, Future Readiness Score, or Future Success Index formula. Raw scores are not combined directly, because the two assessment formats produce different raw ranges.

For the 18 self-report (Likert) dimensions — all Personality, Learning Style, and Career Interest dimensions, plus Creative Thinking and Leadership Potential — the raw range is 6–30:

Normalized Score = ((Raw Score − 6) ÷ 24) × 100

For the 4 hybrid ability-tested dimensions — Verbal Ability, Numerical Ability, Logical Reasoning, and Spatial & Visual Reasoning — the raw range is 0–6 (number of correct answers):

Normalized Score = (Raw Score ÷ 6) × 100

Every weighting formula in Step 4A, and the Future Readiness Score and Future Success Index formulas in Step 3, operate on these Normalized Scores.



2. Bipolar Dimension Scoring Rule

Structured vs Exploratory Learning is a single bipolar dimension measured by one set of 6 questions, producing one raw score. A high score reflects an Exploratory preference; a low score reflects a Structured preference. There is no separate question set for each end.

Where a Theme formula references “Exploratory Learning,” use the dimension’s Normalized Score directly.

Where a Theme formula references “Structured Learning,” use (100 − Normalized Score) — i.e. the inverse of the same dimension.



3. Dimension Name Key

Theme formulas use short names for some dimensions. Each maps to exactly one of the 22 official dimensions defined in the DIMENSION document:

“Curiosity” = Curiosity & Openness

“Adaptability” = Adaptability & Resilience

“Visual Learning” = Visual Learning Preference

“Experiential Learning” = Practical & Experiential Learning

“Spatial Ability” = Spatial & Visual Reasoning

“Structured Learning / Exploratory Learning” = Structured vs Exploratory Learning (see Bipolar Dimension Scoring Rule above)

All other dimension names used in Theme formulas (e.g. Verbal Ability, Numerical Ability, Logical Reasoning, Social Energy, Collaborative Learning, Independent Learning, Creative Thinking, Leadership Potential, Conscientiousness & Discipline, Collaboration & Empathy, and all six Career Interest dimensions) match an official dimension name exactly.

“Problem Solving” and “Persistence,” which appeared in earlier drafts of the Theme formulas, are not separately measured dimensions. They have been resolved into Logical Reasoning and Conscientiousness & Discipline respectively throughout Step 4A, since those dimensions already cover the same underlying trait (logical/analytical problem-solving, and disciplined follow-through). No new dimensions were added to the 22-dimension model.



4. Tie-Breaking Rule for Top 3 Themes

Themes are ranked by Theme Score (0–100, normalized) to determine each student’s Top 3 Themes. If two or more Theme Scores fall within 1 point of each other at the boundary of the Top 3:

Rank the theme with the higher score on its single highest-weighted contributing dimension first.

If still tied after that check, display all tied themes rather than arbitrarily dropping one (the Top 3 becomes a Top 4 only in this rare case).



5. Future Readiness Score — Formula

(Full definition appears under Step 3, Output 9. Repeated here for developer convenience.)

Future Readiness Score=(Adaptability & Resilience × 25%)+(Curiosity & Openness × 20%)+(Conscientiousness & Discipline × 15%)+(Independent Learning × 15%)+(Logical Reasoning × 10%)+(Average of Top 3 Theme Scores × 15%)



6. Future Success Index — Formula

(Full definition appears under Step 3, Output 10. Repeated here for developer convenience.)

Future Success Index=(Conscientiousness & Discipline × 25%)+(Average of Top 3 Theme Scores × 25%)+(Adaptability & Resilience × 20%)+(Collaboration & Empathy × 15%)+(Curiosity & Openness × 15%)

“Average of Top 3 Theme Scores” in both formulas above = the mean of the student’s 3 highest Normalized Theme Scores (after the Tie-Breaking Rule has been applied).
