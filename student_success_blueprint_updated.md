These rules apply uniformly across every formula in this document.</p><p><strong>1. Normalization Rule</strong></p><p>Every dimension score must be converted to a 0–100 Normalized Score before it is used in any Theme Score, Career Fit Score, Future Readiness Score, or Future Success Index formula. Raw scores are not combined directly, because the two assessment formats produce different raw ranges.</p><p>For the 18 self-report (Likert) dimensions — all Personality, Learning Style, and Career Interest dimensions, plus Creative Thinking and Leadership Potential — the raw range is 6–30:</p><p>Normalized Score = ((Raw Score − 6) ÷ 24) × 100</p><p>For the 4 hybrid ability-tested dimensions — Verbal Ability, Numerical Ability, Logical Reasoning, and Spatial &amp; Visual Reasoning — the raw range is 0–6 (number of correct answers):</p><p>Normalized Score = (Raw Score ÷ 6) × 100</p><p>Every weighting formula in Step 4A, and the Future Readiness Score and Future Success Index formulas in Step 3, operate on these Normalized Scores.</p><p><strong>2. Bipolar Dimension Scoring Rule</strong></p><p>Structured vs Exploratory Learning is a single bipolar dimension measured by one set of 6 questions, producing one raw score. A high score reflects an Exploratory preference; a low score reflects a Structured preference. There is no separate question set for each end.</p><p>Where a Theme formula references “Exploratory Learning,” use the dimension’s Normalized Score directly.</p><p>Where a Theme formula references “Structured Learning,” use (100 − Normalized Score) — i.e. the inverse of the same dimension.</p><p><strong>3. Dimension Name Key</strong></p><p>Theme formulas use short names for some dimensions. Each maps to exactly one of the 22 official dimensions defined in the DIMENSION document:</p><ul><li>“Curiosity” = Curiosity &amp; Openness</li><li>“Adaptability” = Adaptability &amp; Resilience</li><li>“Visual Learning” = Visual Learning Preference</li><li>“Experiential Learning” = Practical &amp; Experiential Learning</li><li>“Spatial Ability” = Spatial &amp; Visual Reasoning</li><li>“Structured Learning / Exploratory Learning” = Structured vs Exploratory Learning (see Bipolar Dimension Scoring Rule above)</li></ul><p>All other dimension names used in Theme formulas (e.g. Verbal Ability, Numerical Ability, Logical Reasoning, Social Energy, Collaborative Learning, Independent Learning, Creative Thinking, Leadership Potential, Conscientiousness &amp; Discipline, Collaboration &amp; Empathy, and all six Career Interest dimensions) match an official dimension name exactly.</p><p>“Problem Solving” and “Persistence,” which appeared in earlier drafts of the Theme formulas, are not separately measured dimensions. They have been resolved into Logical Reasoning and Conscientiousness &amp; Discipline respectively throughout Step 4A, since those dimensions already cover the same underlying trait (logical/analytical problem-solving, and disciplined follow-through). No new dimensions were added to the 22-dimension model.</p><p><strong>4. Tie-Breaking Rule for Top 3 Themes</strong></p><p>Themes are ranked by Theme Score (0–100, normalized) to determine each student’s Top 3 Themes. If two or more Theme Scores fall within 1 point of each other at the boundary of the Top 3:</p><ul><li>Rank the theme with the higher score on its single highest-weighted contributing dimension first.</li><li>If still tied after that check, display all tied themes rather than arbitrarily dropping one (the Top 3 becomes a Top 4 only in this rare case).</li></ul><p><strong>5. Future Readiness Score — Formula</strong></p><p>(Full definition appears under Step 3, Output 9.
