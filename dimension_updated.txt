SECTION 1: PERSONALITY PROFILE

DIMENSION 1: SOCIAL ENERGY



DIMENSION PURPOSE

Measures how students naturally interact with people, participate in social situations, build relationships, and derive energy from social interaction or solitude.

This dimension focuses on a student's natural social orientation and interaction style.

It does not measure:

Confidence 

Self-esteem 

Popularity 

Communication ability 

A student can be highly introverted and highly confident, or highly extroverted and socially anxious.



WHAT THIS DIMENSION MEASURES

Social interaction preferences 

Group participation tendencies 

Relationship-building style 

Communication engagement 

Social comfort 

Source of personal energy 

Preference for collaboration or independent work 



WHY THIS DIMENSION MATTERS

Social Energy influences:

Classroom participation 

Teamwork and collaboration 

Leadership tendencies 

Communication style 

Learning preferences 

Relationship-building 

Preferred work environments 

Understanding Social Energy helps students recognize how they naturally engage with others and what environments may allow them to perform at their best.



LOW SCORE TRAITS

Reserved / Introverted Social Style

Students with lower scores typically:

Prefer smaller social circles 

Enjoy spending time alone 

Think before speaking 

Observe before participating 

Build fewer but deeper relationships 

Prefer meaningful conversations over frequent interactions 

Recharge through solitude and personal reflection 

Feel comfortable working independently 

May avoid unnecessary social interaction 

Value privacy and personal space 



MEDIUM SCORE TRAITS

Balanced / Ambivert Social Style

Students with medium scores typically:

Enjoy both social interaction and personal time 

Adapt to different social environments 

Work effectively independently and collaboratively 

Participate when necessary 

Balance interaction and reflection 

Build relationships comfortably 

Communicate appropriately based on context 

Can lead or follow depending on the situation 

Enjoy variety in learning and work settings 



HIGH SCORE TRAITS

Outgoing / Extroverted Social Style

Students with higher scores typically:

Enjoy interacting with people 

Participate actively in groups 

Feel energized by social environments 

Build relationships easily 

Share ideas openly 

Enjoy teamwork and collaboration 

Feel comfortable meeting new people 

Prefer active and engaging environments 

Often take initiative during discussions 

Enjoy networking and social engagement

DIMENSION 4: ENTERPRISING INTEREST

(Based on Holland's RIASEC Career Interest Framework)



DIMENSION PURPOSE

Measures a student's interest in influencing people, leading initiatives, building ideas, taking initiative, persuading others, creating opportunities, and driving results.

This dimension evaluates attraction toward leadership, business, entrepreneurship, management, and influence-oriented activities.

This dimension focuses on career interests, not leadership ability.

This dimension does not measure:

Leadership Potential 

Confidence 

Extroversion 

Business skills 

A student can have high Enterprising Interest even if they have never led a team or started a project.



WHAT THIS DIMENSION MEASURES

Interest in leadership 

Entrepreneurship interest 

Business interest 

Influence and persuasion 

Initiative-taking 

Opportunity creation 

Decision-making interest 

Goal-oriented activities 

Competitive drive 

Achievement motivation 



WHY THIS DIMENSION MATTERS

Students with strong Enterprising Interest are often drawn toward:

Leadership opportunities 

Business and entrepreneurship 

Influencing others 

Decision-making roles 

Project ownership 

Building and growing initiatives 

This dimension helps identify whether students naturally enjoy taking charge, influencing outcomes, and creating impact.



LOW SCORE TRAITS

Specialist / Contributor Interest Style

Students with lower scores typically:

Prefer contributing expertise rather than leading others 

Enjoy technical or specialist work 

Prefer working on tasks rather than managing people 

Avoid highly competitive environments 

Prefer stability over risk-taking 

Focus on quality rather than influence 

Feel comfortable supporting existing systems 



MEDIUM SCORE TRAITS

Balanced Influence Interest Style

Students with medium scores typically:

Enjoy leadership in some situations 

Comfortable taking initiative when needed 

Balance personal contribution and leadership 

Enjoy responsibility without constantly seeking it 

Adapt to different team roles 



HIGH SCORE TRAITS

Leadership & Enterprise Interest Style

Students with higher scores typically:

Enjoy leading projects and initiatives 

Like influencing decisions and outcomes 

Feel motivated by achievement and impact 

Enjoy entrepreneurship and business-related activities

Prefer environments where they can create change
